MISSION STATEMENT

The Directorate of Cyber Operations serves as the principal cyber intelligence and operations arm of AfghanNet Holdings, responsible for foreign signals collection, defensive cyber operations, counterintelligence, and strategic impact missions in support of Afghan national security interests.

ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

Office of Signals Intelligence

(OSI) · Foreign signals collection and analysis

Mandate: Collect, process, and disseminate foreign communications and electronic signals of intelligence value to Afghan national security.

Capabilities
  • Strategic signals collection against regional threats
  • Technical exploitation of foreign communications networks
  • Cryptanalytic capabilities against targeted systems
  • Early warning systems for emerging cyber threats

Operational Focus: Regional state actors, non-state threats, and foreign intelligence services operating in or targeting Afghan interests.

Cyber Defense Command

(CDC) · Defensive cyber operations for national infrastructure

Mandate: Protect Afghan government networks, critical infrastructure, and sovereign digital assets from foreign intrusion and cyber attack.

Capabilities
  • 24/7 network monitoring and threat detection
  • M9-ASSASSIN defensive platform deployment
  • Incident response and remediation
  • Vulnerability assessment and penetration testing of domestic systems

Operational Focus: Government networks, military systems, energy infrastructure, financial sector, and telecommunications backbone.

Office of Counter Intelligence

(OCI) · Identify and neutralize foreign intelligence threats

Mandate: Detect, investigate, and counter foreign intelligence activities directed against Afghan interests and AfghanNet operations.

Capabilities
  • Counterintelligence investigations
  • Double agent operations
  • Foreign intelligence service penetration
  • Insider threat detection and mitigation

Operational Focus: Foreign intelligence officers, recruited assets, and technical surveillance operations targeting Afghan sovereignty.

Strategic Impact Division

(SID) · Offensive cyber operations

Mandate: Conduct computer network operations to disrupt, deny, or degrade hostile foreign capabilities targeting Afghan interests.

Capabilities
  • K9-ASSASSIN platform deployment
  • Targeted disruption of hostile infrastructure
  • Precision cyber effects against validated threats
  • Retaliatory operations when authorized

Operational Focus: Nation-state adversaries, terrorist networks, and entities actively targeting Afghan national security.

Foreign Liaison Office

(FLO)

Mandate: Establish and maintain information-sharing partnerships with friendly foreign intelligence and security services.

  • Bilateral intelligence sharing
  • Joint ops coordination
  • Technical exchange

Technical Operations Group

(TOG)

Mandate: R&D of advanced technical capabilities for DCO.

  • Custom surveillance tech
  • Exploit development
  • Quantum-resistant crypto

Human Intelligence Division

(HID)

Mandate: Recruit and manage human assets.

  • Asset recruitment
  • Covert action support
  • Defector debriefing

Analysis & Production Center

(APC)

Mandate: All-source intelligence fusion for Afghan leadership.

  • Multi-source fusion
  • Predictive analysis
  • Threat assessment

Special Collection Service

(SCS)

Mandate: Unacknowledged and sensitive collection ops.

  • Denied area collection
  • Black bag operations
  • Special access programs

Office of the Inspector General

(OIG)

Mandate: Internal oversight and accountability.

  • Compliance reviews
  • Whistleblower protection
  • Independent reporting
